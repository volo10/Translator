# Bilingual Proficiency Skill: Hebrew to English

## Overview
Deep knowledge of Hebrew and English for accurate translation.

## Fundamental Differences

### Writing Direction
- **Hebrew:** Right-to-left
- **English:** Left-to-right

### Word Formation
- **Hebrew:** Root-based (3-letter roots + patterns)
- **English:** Linear construction
- Example: כ-ת-ב (k-t-b) root → many writing-related words

### Morphology
- **Hebrew:** Highly inflected (prefixes, suffixes, infixes)
- **English:** Less inflected, uses word order

## Core Vocabulary Mappings

### Common Words
- כן/לא → yes/no
- טוב/רע → good/bad
- גדול/קטן → big/small
- איש/אישה → man/woman
- יום/לילה → day/night

### Hebrew Loan Words in English
- kibbutz, sabra, chutzpah, kosher

## Verb Systems

### Hebrew Binyanim → English Verbs
**Pa'al (פעל):** Basic active
- כתב (katav) → wrote

**Nif'al (נפעל):** Passive/reflexive
- נכתב (nikhtav) → was written

**Pi'el (פיעל):** Intensive
- דיבר (diber) → spoke

**Pu'al (פועל):** Intensive passive
- דובר (dubar) → was spoken

**Hitpa'el (התפעל):** Reflexive/reciprocal
- התכתב (hitkhtev) → corresponded

**Hif'il (הפעיל):** Causative
- הכתיב (hiktiv) → dictated

**Huf'al (הֻפְעַל):** Causative passive
- הוכתב (hukhtav) → was dictated

### Tense Mapping

**Hebrew Past → English Past**
- דיברתי (dibarti) → I spoke

**Hebrew Present → English Present/Progressive**
- אני מדבר (ani medaber) → I speak / I am speaking

**Hebrew Future → English Future**
- אדבר (adaber) → I will speak

**No Hebrew Present Perfect:**
- Use past tense + time markers
- דיברתי כבר (dibarti kvar) → I have already spoken

## Gender System

**Hebrew:** Masculine/feminine (no neuter)
- All nouns have gender
- Verbs agree with subject gender
- Adjectives agree with noun gender

**English:** 
- No grammatical gender for most nouns
- Gender only in pronouns (he/she/it)
- No adjective agreement

**Translation Impact:**
- Hebrew מדבר/מדברת → both "speaks" in English
- Must determine pronoun (he/she) from Hebrew gender

## Plural Forms

**Hebrew:**
- Masculine: usually ים- (-im)
- Feminine: usually ות- (-ot)
- Dual for pairs: ידיים (yadayim)
- Many irregular plurals

**English:**
- Add -s or -es
- Some irregular (child/children, man/men)
- No dual form

## Articles

**Hebrew:**
- Definite: ה- (ha-) prefix
- No indefinite article

**English:**
- Definite: "the"
- Indefinite: "a/an"

**Translation:**
- הספר (ha-sefer) → the book
- ספר (sefer) → a book

## Possession

**Hebrew:**
- Possessive suffixes: ספרי (sifri - my book)
- של (shel): הספר שלי (ha-sefer sheli)
- Construct state (smichut): ספר יוחנן

**English:**
- Possessive adjectives: my, your, his, her
- Possessive 's: John's book
- Of construction: book of John

## Sentence Structure

**Hebrew:** Flexible, SVO common in modern, VSO in literary
**English:** Rigid SVO

- אני אוכל תפוח → I eat an apple (both SVO)

## Object Markers

**Hebrew:** את (et) before definite objects
- אני רואה את הספר (ani ro'e et ha-sefer)

**English:** No object marker
- I see the book

## Prepositions

**Hebrew Prefixes:**
- ב- (be-) → in, at, with
- ל- (le-) → to, for
- מ- (mi-) → from

**English:** Separate words
- בבית (ba-bayit) → in the house
- לבית (la-bayit) → to the house
- מהבית (me-ha-bayit) → from the house

## Pronouns

### Subject Pronouns

**Hebrew:**
- אני (ani) - I
- אתה/את (ata/at) - you (m/f)
- הוא/היא (hu/hi) - he/she
- אנחנו (anakhnu) - we
- אתם/אתן (atem/aten) - you (m.pl/f.pl)
- הם/הן (hem/hen) - they (m/f)

**English:**
- I, you, he/she/it, we, you (pl), they

**Key difference:** Hebrew has gender in all persons; English only in 3rd singular

## Negation

**Hebrew:** לא (lo) before verb
**English:** not after auxiliary or BE

- אני לא מדבר → I don't speak
- אני לא רופא → I'm not a doctor

## Questions

**Hebrew:** 
- Intonation or האם (ha'im)
- Question words: מה, מי, איפה, מתי, למה, איך

**English:**
- Auxiliary verb + subject inversion
- Question words: what, who, where, when, why, how

**Translation:**
- ?אתה מדבר אנגלית → Do you speak English?
- ?מה אתה רוצה → What do you want?

## Adjectives

**Hebrew:**
- After noun
- Agrees in gender/number
- Takes ה when noun is definite

**English:**
- Before noun
- No agreement

**Translation:**
- הבית הלבן → the white house
- בית לבן → a white house

## Number Inflection

**Hebrew:** Numbers have gender (opposite of noun!)
**English:** Numbers invariable

- שלושה ספרים (m) → three books
- שלוש מכוניות (f) → three cars
- Both → "three" in English

## Existential Constructions

**Hebrew:**
- יש (yesh) - there is/are
- אין (ein) - there isn't/aren't
- היה (haya) - there was
- יהיה (yihiye) - there will be

**English:**
- there is/are, there was/were, there will be

## Relative Clauses

**Hebrew:** ש- (she-) prefix attached
- הספר שקראתי → the book that I read

**English:** that, which, who (separate words)

## Passive Voice

**Hebrew:** Passive binyanim very common
- הספר נכתב → The book was written

**English:** be + past participle (less common)

## Time Expressions

### Days
- יום ראשון...יום שישי, שבת → Sunday...Friday, Saturday

### Time
- השעה שלוש → It's three o'clock

### Markers
- היום/אתמול/מחר → today/yesterday/tomorrow
- עכשיו → now
- אחר כך → later

## Formality

**Hebrew:**
- Less formal register distinction
- Same pronouns for formal/informal
- Formality through vocabulary

**English:**
- Formality through vocabulary and style
- "you" for all contexts

## Modern Hebrew Characteristics

- Many English loan words
- Slang from multiple languages
- Acronyms common
- Casual, direct style

## Translation Guidelines

When translating Hebrew to English:
1. **Identify verb binyan** to understand meaning nuance
2. **Check noun gender** to select correct pronouns
3. **Handle definiteness** (ה- → "the" or "a/an")
4. **Convert prefix prepositions** to English words
5. **Remove את** (no equivalent in English)
6. **Position adjectives** before nouns
7. **Add articles** where Hebrew has none
8. **Simplify verb structures** (binyanim → English verbs)
9. **Use progressive** where appropriate
10. **Maintain natural English** word order
