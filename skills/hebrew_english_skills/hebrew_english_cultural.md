# Cultural/Pragmatic Competence: Hebrew to English

## Israeli Culture → English

### Communication Style
**Hebrew/Israeli:** Direct, informal (dugri)
**English (varies):** May be more indirect
- Translation may need slight softening

### Formality
**Hebrew:** Generally informal
**English:** More formality registers
- "אתה יכול..." → "Could you..." (add politeness)

### Idioms

**Hebrew Idioms → English:**
- "ראש גדול" (big head) → headache/problem
- "לפגוע בול" (hit bullseye) → hit the nail on the head
- "לעצום עין" (close eye) → turn a blind eye
- "לשפוך שמן על האש" (pour oil on fire) → add fuel to the fire

### Cultural References

**Israeli Context:**
- Military terms (צה"ל, מילואים)
- Jewish holidays (חג, פסח, יום כיפור)
- Israeli geography
- May need brief explanation

**Israeli Slang:**
- סבבה (sababa) → cool/okay/fine
- יאללה (yalla) → let's go/come on
- אחלה (akhla) → great/awesome
- חוצפה (khutzpa) → nerve/audacity
- בלאגן (balagan) → mess/chaos

### Business Communication
**Hebrew:** Can be quite direct
**English:** May need more formal opening/closing
- "שלום," → "Dear" or "Hello"
- "בברכה" → "Best regards" / "Sincerely"

### Greetings
- "מה נשמע" (ma nishma) → "What's up" / "How are you"
- "שלום" → "Hello" / "Hi" / "Goodbye" (context)
- "בסדר" (beseder) → "okay" / "fine" / "alright"

### Requests
**Hebrew:** Direct often acceptable
**English:** May need softening
- "תעשה" (do) → "Could you do" / "Please do"

### Time Expressions
- "מחר" (makhar) → tomorrow
- "אחר כך" (akhar kakh) → later
- Both cultures have flexible time, but translate literally

### Religious/Cultural Terms
- "ברוך השם" (baruch hashem) → "thank God"
- "אם ירצה השם" (im yirtze hashem) → "God willing"
- May keep or translate depending on context

## Translation Strategy

### Direct → Polite
Consider softening Israeli directness:
- Command → polite request
- Direct statement → softer phrasing

### Slang → English Equivalent
Find natural English equivalent:
- "סבבה" → "cool" / "okay" / "sounds good"

### Cultural Notes
Add when helpful:
- Holidays: brief explanation if needed
- Terms: short clarification if obscure

### Register Matching
- Informal Hebrew → informal English
- Formal Hebrew → formal English (though Hebrew less formal overall)

## Best Practices
1. **Understand Israeli directness** isn't rude
2. **Find English idioms** for Hebrew ones
3. **Soften if needed** for English audience
4. **Translate slang naturally**
5. **Brief notes** for cultural references when needed
6. **Match register** to source text
7. **Maintain tone** while adapting for English norms
