# Bilingual Proficiency Skill

## Overview
This skill provides deep knowledge of both English and Spanish languages, including vocabulary, grammar, syntax, and linguistic structures necessary for accurate translation.

## Core Competencies

### 1. Vocabulary Knowledge

#### English-Spanish Core Vocabulary Mappings
- **Common words**: yes/no → sí/no, good/bad → bueno/malo, big/small → grande/pequeño
- **False friends** (palabras engañosas):
  - "embarazada" = pregnant (NOT embarrassed = avergonzado/a)
  - "actually" = en realidad (NOT actualmente = currently)
  - "constipated" = estreñido (NOT constipado = congested/having a cold)
  - "exit" = salida (NOT éxito = success)
  - "carpet" = alfombra (NOT carpeta = folder)
  - "sensible" = sensato (NOT sensible = sensitive/sensitivo)

#### Register and Formality Levels
- **Formal**: usted, señor/señora, would you be so kind → ¿sería tan amable?
- **Informal**: tú, vos (in some regions), hey → oye/hey
- **Professional**: estimado/a, atentos saludos, cordialmente
- **Colloquial**: tío/tía (Spain), wey/güey (Mexico), che (Argentina)

### 2. Grammatical Structures

#### Verb Conjugation System
**Present Tense Regular Verbs:**
- -AR verbs (hablar): hablo, hablas, habla, hablamos, habláis, hablan
- -ER verbs (comer): como, comes, come, comemos, coméis, comen
- -IR verbs (vivir): vivo, vives, vive, vivimos, vivís, viven

**Key Irregular Verbs:**
- ser (to be): soy, eres, es, somos, sois, son
- estar (to be): estoy, estás, está, estamos, estáis, están
- tener (to have): tengo, tienes, tiene, tenemos, tenéis, tienen
- ir (to go): voy, vas, va, vamos, vais, van
- hacer (to do/make): hago, haces, hace, hacemos, hacéis, hacen

**Tense Usage:**
- Present: actions happening now or habitual actions
- Preterite: completed actions in the past (hablé, comí, viví)
- Imperfect: ongoing past actions, descriptions (hablaba, comía, vivía)
- Future: will do something (hablaré, comeré, viviré)
- Conditional: would do something (hablaría, comería, viviría)
- Present perfect: have done (he hablado, he comido, he vivido)
- Subjunctive: uncertainty, wishes, emotions (hable, coma, viva)

#### Ser vs Estar (Critical Distinction)
**Use SER for:**
- Identity: Soy Rafael (I am Rafael)
- Characteristics: Es inteligente (He/She is intelligent)
- Origin: Soy de Israel (I am from Israel)
- Profession: Es ingeniero (He is an engineer)
- Time: Son las tres (It's three o'clock)
- Material: Es de madera (It's made of wood)
- Possession: Es mío (It's mine)

**Use ESTAR for:**
- Location: Estoy en casa (I am at home)
- Temporary states: Estoy cansado (I am tired)
- Emotions: Está feliz (He/She is happy)
- Progressive tenses: Estoy trabajando (I am working)
- Results of actions: La puerta está abierta (The door is open)

#### Gender and Number Agreement
**Noun Gender Rules:**
- Typically -o endings = masculine (el libro, el perro)
- Typically -a endings = feminine (la casa, la mesa)
- Exceptions: el día, el mapa, la mano, la foto
- Invariant endings: -e (el/la estudiante), -ista (el/la artista)

**Adjective Agreement:**
- Must match noun in gender and number
- el chico alto, la chica alta
- los chicos altos, las chicas altas
- Invariant adjectives: inteligente, fuerte, grande (shortened to gran before singular nouns)

#### Pronoun System
**Subject Pronouns:**
- yo (I), tú (you informal), él/ella/usted (he/she/you formal)
- nosotros/nosotras (we), vosotros/vosotras (you plural Spain), ellos/ellas/ustedes (they/you plural)

**Object Pronouns:**
- Direct: me, te, lo/la, nos, os, los/las
- Indirect: me, te, le, nos, os, les
- Placement: before conjugated verbs or attached to infinitives/gerunds/commands

**Reflexive Pronouns:**
- me, te, se, nos, os, se
- Used with reflexive verbs: levantarse (to get up), ducharse (to shower)

### 3. Syntax and Word Order

#### Standard Word Order
- Basic: Subject + Verb + Object (SVO)
  - English: "I eat apples"
  - Spanish: "Yo como manzanas"

#### Flexible Word Order
Spanish allows more flexibility than English:
- Questions can maintain SVO: "¿Tú comes manzanas?" or invert: "¿Comes tú manzanas?"
- Emphasis through word order: "Las manzanas como yo" (emphasizes apples)

#### Adjective Placement
**Before noun (descriptive/subjective):**
- una buena idea (a good idea)
- un viejo amigo (an old friend - long-time)

**After noun (objective/distinguishing):**
- un coche rojo (a red car)
- un amigo viejo (an old friend - elderly)

**Adjectives that change meaning by position:**
- grande: gran casa (great house) vs casa grande (big house)
- pobre: pobre hombre (unfortunate man) vs hombre pobre (poor man - financially)
- nuevo: nuevo coche (new car - new to owner) vs coche nuevo (brand new car)

#### Negative Construction
- Single negative with "no" before verb: "No como carne" (I don't eat meat)
- Double negatives are correct: "No veo nada" (I don't see anything - literally "I don't see nothing")
- Other negatives: nunca (never), nadie (nobody), ninguno (none), tampoco (neither)

### 4. Prepositions

#### Common Preposition Usage
- **a**: to, at (direction, time) - Voy a Madrid, a las tres
  - Personal "a": Veo a María (I see María)
- **de**: of, from (possession, origin) - el libro de Juan, soy de España
- **en**: in, on, at (location, time) - Estoy en casa, en enero
- **con**: with - Voy con Pedro
- **sin**: without - Café sin azúcar
- **para**: for (purpose, destination, deadline) - Es para ti, salgo para México
- **por**: for, by, through (reason, means, duration) - por ti, por teléfono, por dos horas

#### Por vs Para (Critical Distinction)
**Use POR for:**
- Reason/motive: Lo hice por ti (I did it for/because of you)
- Duration: Estudié por dos horas (I studied for two hours)
- Means/method: Hablamos por teléfono (We talked by phone)
- Through/along: Caminé por el parque (I walked through the park)
- Exchange: Pagué 20 euros por el libro (I paid 20 euros for the book)
- "Per": 60 km por hora (60 km per hour)

**Use PARA for:**
- Purpose: Estudio para aprender (I study in order to learn)
- Destination: Salgo para España (I'm leaving for Spain)
- Recipient: Este regalo es para ti (This gift is for you)
- Deadline: Es para mañana (It's for/due tomorrow)
- Opinion: Para mí, es difícil (For me/In my opinion, it's difficult)
- Employment: Trabajo para Google (I work for Google)

### 5. Articles

#### Definite Articles (the)
- el (masculine singular): el libro
- la (feminine singular): la casa
- los (masculine plural): los libros
- las (feminine plural): las casas

**Special cases:**
- el agua (feminine noun starting with stressed a)
- Use with: languages (el español), days (el lunes), possessive expressions (me duele la cabeza)

#### Indefinite Articles (a/an)
- un (masculine): un libro
- una (feminine): una casa
- unos (some, masculine plural): unos libros
- unas (some, feminine plural): unas casas

**Omission of articles:**
- After "ser" with professions: Soy médico (I'm a doctor)
- With "otro": otra vez (another time)
- In negative sentences: No tengo coche (I don't have a car)

### 6. Key Linguistic Differences

#### Progressive Forms
- English: I am eating
- Spanish: Estoy comiendo (using gerund -ando/-iendo)
- Note: Spanish uses simple present more often for ongoing actions

#### Possession
- English: apostrophe 's (John's car)
- Spanish: de + possessor (el coche de Juan)
- Possessive adjectives: mi/tu/su (my/your/his-her-its), nuestro/vuestro/su (our/your plural/their)

#### Questions
- Inverted question marks: ¿Cómo estás?
- Common question words: qué (what), quién (who), dónde (where), cuándo (when), por qué (why), cómo (how), cuánto (how much/many)

#### Diminutives and Augmentatives
- Diminutives (-ito/-ita): pequeñito (tiny), gatito (kitten) - express smallness or affection
- Augmentatives (-ón/-ona, -ote/-ota): grandote (very big), cabezón (big-headed)

### 7. Common Verb Phrases and Constructions

#### Gustar-Type Verbs (Indirect Object + Verb + Subject)
Structure: The thing is pleasing to the person
- Me gusta el café (I like coffee - literally: Coffee is pleasing to me)
- Te gustan los libros (You like books)
- Similar verbs: encantar (to love), interesar (to interest), importar (to matter), molestar (to bother)

#### Infinitive Constructions
- Ir a + infinitive (going to): Voy a estudiar (I'm going to study)
- Tener que + infinitive (to have to): Tengo que trabajar (I have to work)
- Poder + infinitive (can): Puedo ayudar (I can help)
- Querer + infinitive (want to): Quiero comer (I want to eat)
- Deber + infinitive (should/must): Debo dormir (I should sleep)
- Acabar de + infinitive (just did): Acabo de llegar (I just arrived)

### 8. Numbers and Quantities

#### Cardinal Numbers
- 0-10: cero, uno, dos, tres, cuatro, cinco, seis, siete, ocho, nueve, diez
- 11-15: once, doce, trece, catorce, quince
- 16-19: dieciséis, diecisiete, dieciocho, diecinueve
- 20-29: veinte, veintiuno, veintidós, etc.
- 30, 40, 50...: treinta, cuarenta, cincuenta, sesenta, setenta, ochenta, noventa
- 100: cien (before nouns), ciento (before other numbers)
- 1000: mil
- Note: "uno" becomes "un" before masculine nouns

#### Ordinal Numbers
- 1st-10th: primero, segundo, tercero, cuarto, quinto, sexto, séptimo, octavo, noveno, décimo
- Agreement with gender: primer piso, primera vez

### 9. Time Expressions

#### Telling Time
- ¿Qué hora es? (What time is it?)
- Es la una (It's one o'clock)
- Son las dos/tres/cuatro... (It's two/three/four o'clock)
- Son las tres y media (3:30)
- Son las cuatro y cuarto (4:15)
- Son las cinco menos cuarto (4:45)

#### Time References
- ahora (now), luego (later), después (after), antes (before)
- hoy (today), ayer (yesterday), mañana (tomorrow)
- esta mañana/tarde/noche (this morning/afternoon/evening)
- la semana pasada (last week), el año próximo (next year)

### 10. Regional Variations

#### Major Dialect Differences
**Spain (Castilian):**
- Vosotros form used (you plural informal)
- Theta pronunciation (z, c before e/i)
- Use of "coger" (to take/catch)

**Latin America:**
- Ustedes for all "you plural" contexts
- Seseo (z, c sound like s)
- Regional vocabulary variations

**Voseo (Argentina, Uruguay, parts of Central America):**
- Vos instead of tú
- Different conjugations: vos hablás, vos comés, vos vivís

#### Regional Vocabulary Examples
- Car: coche (Spain), carro (Latin America), auto (various)
- Computer: ordenador (Spain), computadora (Latin America)
- To drive: conducir (Spain), manejar (Latin America)
- Cell phone: móvil (Spain), celular (Latin America)

## Application Guidelines

### When Translating
1. **Identify the source language structures** clearly
2. **Map vocabulary** with attention to false friends
3. **Apply correct grammatical gender and number**
4. **Choose appropriate verb tenses** based on context
5. **Consider register** (formal vs informal)
6. **Maintain natural Spanish syntax**, not word-for-word translation
7. **Account for regional preferences** if target audience is specified

### Quality Checks
- Verify verb conjugations match subject
- Confirm gender/number agreement for all adjectives
- Check preposition usage (especially por/para, a/en)
- Ensure ser/estar distinction is correct
- Validate that pronouns are used appropriately
- Confirm natural word order for Spanish

## Common Translation Patterns

### Subject Omission
Spanish often omits subject pronouns when clear from context:
- "I am happy" → "Estoy feliz" (not "Yo estoy feliz" unless emphasizing)

### Reflexive Verbs
Many verbs are reflexive in Spanish but not English:
- "to go to sleep" → "dormirse"
- "to wake up" → "despertarse"
- "to get up" → "levantarse"

### Progressive Tenses
Use sparingly; simple present often preferred:
- "I'm reading" can be "Leo" or "Estoy leyendo" depending on emphasis

### Passive Voice
Less common in Spanish; use active voice or "se" constructions:
- "It is said" → "Se dice"
- "Spanish is spoken" → "Se habla español"
