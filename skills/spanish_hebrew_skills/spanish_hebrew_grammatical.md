# Grammatical and Syntactic Awareness: Spanish to Hebrew

## Overview
Understanding structural differences between Spanish and Hebrew for proper translation.

## Major Structural Transformations

### 1. Word Order
**Spanish:** Primarily SVO, flexible
**Hebrew:** SVO in modern, VSO in literary
- "Yo como manzanas" → "אני אוכל תפוחים"
- Modern Hebrew prefers SVO like Spanish

### 2. Article System
**Spanish:** Separate articles (el, la, un, una)
**Hebrew:** Prefix ה- for definite, nothing for indefinite
- "el libro" → "הספר" (ha-sefer)
- "un libro" → "ספר" (sefer)

### 3. Adjective Placement
**Both:** Adjectives after nouns
**Hebrew difference:** Adjective takes ה when noun is definite
- "la casa blanca" → "הבית הלבן" (ha-bayit ha-lavan)
- Note both words get ה-

### 4. Possession
**Spanish:** mi, tu, su OR de + possessor
**Hebrew:** Suffix OR של (shel)
- "mi libro" → "ספרי" (sifri) or "הספר שלי" (ha-sefer sheli)
- "el libro de Juan" → "הספר של יוחנן" (ha-sefer shel Yokhanan)

### 5. Prepositions
**Spanish:** Separate words
**Hebrew:** Often prefixes
- "en la casa" → "בבית" (ba-bayit) - ב+ה fuses
- "a la escuela" → "לבית הספר" (le-beit ha-sefer)
- "del libro" → "מהספר" (me-ha-sefer)

### 6. Object Markers
**Spanish:** Personal "a"
**Hebrew:** את (et) for ALL definite objects
- "Veo a María" → "אני רואה את מריה" (ani ro'e et Marya)
- "Veo el libro" → "אני רואה את הספר" (ani ro'e et ha-sefer)

### 7. Verb System Transformation
**Spanish Tenses → Hebrew Binyanim + Tenses**

**Present:**
- "hablo" → "מדבר" (medaber) - Pa'al present, masculine
- Gender/number in Hebrew present: medaber/medaberet/medabrim/medabrot

**Past:**
- "hablé" → "דיברתי" (dibarti) - Pi'el past
- "hablaba" → "דיברתי" (same form, aspect from context)

**Future:**
- "hablaré" → "אדבר" (adaber) - Pi'el future

**Progressive:**
- "estoy hablando" → "אני מדבר" (ani medaber) - simple present

**Perfect:**
- "he hablado" → "דיברתי" (dibarti) - no separate perfect tense

### 8. Passive Voice
**Spanish:** ser + past participle OR se construction
**Hebrew:** Passive binyanim (Nif'al, Pu'al, Huf'al)
- "fue escrito" → "נכתב" (nikhtav) - Nif'al passive
- "se habla español" → "מדברים ספרדית" (medabrim sfardit)

### 9. Reflexive Verbs
**Spanish:** Very common (levantarse, ducharse)
**Hebrew:** Hitpa'el binyan less common
- "me levanto" → "אני קם" (ani kam) - simple verb
- "me ducho" → "אני מתקלח" (ani mitkaleakh) - Hitpa'el

### 10. Negation
**Spanish:** "no" before verb
**Hebrew:** "לא" (lo) before verb
- "No hablo" → "אני לא מדבר" (ani lo medaber)
- Both languages straightforward

### 11. Questions
**Spanish:** Intonation, inverted question marks
**Hebrew:** Intonation OR האם (ha'im)
- "¿Hablas hebreo?" → "אתה מדבר עברית?" (ata medaber ivrit?)
- "¿Hablas hebreo?" → "האם אתה מדבר עברית?" (formal)

### 12. Relative Clauses
**Spanish:** que, quien, cual
**Hebrew:** ש- (she-) prefix
- "el libro que leí" → "הספר שקראתי" (ha-sefer she-karati)
- Much more compact in Hebrew

### 13. Number Agreement
**Spanish:** Singular/plural
**Hebrew:** Singular/plural + dual (pairs)
- "dos manos" → "שתי ידיים" (shtei yadayim) - dual form
- "dos libros" → "שני ספרים" (shnei sfarim) - regular plural

### 14. Gender Transformation
**Critical:** Gender often differs!
- "la mesa" (f) → "השולחן" (m - shulkhan)
- "el sol" (m) → "השמש" (f - shemesh)
- All modifiers must match HEBREW gender, not Spanish

### 15. Pronoun Placement
**Spanish:** After verb (lo veo)
**Hebrew:** Separate word with את
- "lo veo" → "אני רואה אותו" (ani ro'e oto)
- Not a clitic in Hebrew

### 16. Subjunctive Mood
**Spanish:** Extensive subjunctive system
**Hebrew:** No subjunctive; use future/present
- "Quiero que vengas" → "אני רוצה שתבוא" (ani rotze she-tavo)
- ש + future tense replaces subjunctive

### 17. Infinitive Constructions
**Spanish:** Common with modal verbs
**Hebrew:** ל + infinitive OR ש + future
- "Quiero comer" → "אני רוצה לאכול" (ani rotze le'ekhol)
- "Quiero que comas" → "אני רוצה שתאכל" (ani rotze she-tokhal)

### 18. Comparison Structures
**Spanish:** más/menos... que
**Hebrew:** יותר/פחות (yoter/pakhot)... מ- (mi-)
- "más grande que" → "יותר גדול מ-" (yoter gadol mi-)
- Hebrew uses prefix מ- instead of separate word

### 19. Existential Constructions
**Spanish:** hay/había/habrá
**Hebrew:** יש/היה/יהיה (yesh/haya/yihiye)
- "Hay un libro" → "יש ספר" (yesh sefer)
- Similar structure to Spanish

### 20. Conditional Sentences
**Spanish:** si + tense combinations
**Hebrew:** אם (im) + tense
- "Si llueve, me quedo" → "אם ירד גשם, אני אשאר" (im yered geshem, ani esh'ar)

## Transformation Checklist

Before translating to Hebrew:
- [ ] Identify all verbs and determine binyan needed
- [ ] Check gender of all nouns (use Hebrew gender!)
- [ ] Map prepositions to prefixes where appropriate
- [ ] Handle definiteness (add ה- or omit)
- [ ] Position adjectives with proper agreement
- [ ] Add את before definite objects
- [ ] Convert subjunctive to future tense
- [ ] Transform passive to Hebrew binyan
- [ ] Handle possession (suffix or של)
- [ ] Add subject pronouns (Hebrew less pro-drop)
- [ ] Convert progressive to simple present
- [ ] Map relative clauses to ש- constructions
- [ ] Apply plural forms (including dual if appropriate)
- [ ] Ensure all gender/number agreement with Hebrew nouns

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Keeping Spanish gender** - Must use Hebrew gender
2. **Word-for-word articles** - Hebrew uses prefixes
3. **Omitting את** - Required for definite objects
4. **Missing ה on adjectives** - When noun is definite
5. **Wrong binyan choice** - Affects meaning significantly
6. **Subjunctive literally** - Use future instead
7. **Clitic pronouns** - Hebrew uses separate words
8. **Missing preposition prefixes** - Combine ב, ל, מ with words
